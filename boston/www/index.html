PyLadies Boston

We are a group of women developers who love the Python programming language. Some of us hack on Python projects on the side, some work full-time on Python development, and some are just starting to learn. We host biweekly beginner meetups, monthly presentation and tutorial nights, and quarterly hack days. Free and open to all who identify as women.

Featured Bio

Anne Thessen

1) What is your background?
I have a PhD in biological oceanography from the University of Maryland. I worked for the Encyclopedia of Life and the Data Conservancy at the Marine Biological Laboratory in Woods Hole. I recently started my own data discovery and management company called The Data Detektiv.

2) Why did you join PyLadies?
My programming skills are entirely self-taught. Python is a great language for a beginner because 1. it is easy and 2. there is a huge support system for newbies. I was already a member of Boston Python and learned a lot from their workshops and project nights, but I joined PyLadies also so that I could have additional networking opportunities and attend additional events for beginners.

3) What are you most excited about creating with Python?
I've been working with the Encyclopedia of Life to perform knowledge extraction on their content. I've had some success creating a "digital ecosystem" by extracting species association information from text. I'm really excited to take it to the next step with NLTK and machine learning.
